Board Guide

How To Use The Ranking

Use the board to compare runners, open individual profiles, and cast one vote on each athlete by choosing either an upvote or a downvote. Search can narrow the field by runner, meet, year, or mark, and the event-time menu can isolate athletes who have a recorded performance in the selected event.

The board also includes feedback tools for future updates. Use the profile suggestion form to submit missing marks, results, or corrections for existing runners, and use the Coming Soon suggestion slots at the bottom of the board to recommend athletes who should be considered for addition.

Rankings should be based on distance-running quality rather than popularity. The strongest cases should come from track and cross country distance racing, major race victories, historically strong times, and meaningful versatility across distances and surfaces.

  • Prioritize distance events on the track and in cross country.
  • Reward championship-level wins and other major race victories.
  • Give substantial weight to elite times and historically significant marks.
  • Credit athletes who showed range across multiple distance events.

Browse the board, open a runner profile, and push athletes up or down the list. Shared totals can update live across the public board.
